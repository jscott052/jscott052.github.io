Deciphering Tabs

There seems to be a tab on the internet for pretty much every song out there. Learning how to read tabs can enable you to playing all of them!


Tabs are a great way for beginners to learn how to learn many songs. In the beginning, learning to play more advanced songs by ear can be very difficult for guitarists. Although it's a good idea to train your ear for the future, tabs will help you to learn your favorite songs quite easily!

Above is an example of some tabs you may see.

Tabs follow a very simple format, the Low E string is on the bottom, and the High E string in on top. They read from left to right, and each number indicates where you should be fretting.

There are a couple of symbols that tabs use to represent a certain technique. Below is a list of some of the symbols you may see while reading tabs.

  • H: Hammer On
  • P: Pull-Off
  • B: Bend
  • PM: Palm Mute

Although many tabs can be great learning materials, there are many inaccurate tabs on the internet! Always be sure to check the rating of the tab, if available. Tabs can also sometimes make a song appear to be more difficult than it actually is. Seeing chords in tab form looks much more intimidating rather than seeing a "C" for C Chord.
