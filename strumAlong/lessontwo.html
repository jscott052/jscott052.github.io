Five Essential Chords

Now that you know the parts of your guitar, its time to start playing it! In this lesson you will learn 5 essential open chords.


Chord Diagrams

Before you learn to play the chords, you need to learn how to read a chord diagram.

Pictured on the right is an example of a chord diagram. The vertical lines represent the strings of the guitar, the horizontal strings represent the frets. The darker black line is the starting fret position, it usually starts at the first fret unless it indicates otherwise. Each dot shows where you should place your fingers, and the numbers represent which finger you should use in doing so. Your fingers are numbered one through four, starting from your index finger.



The Chords

The first chords that you will learn are C, A, G, E and D. These are considered Open chords, because they include strumming unfretted strings along with pressed strings. Refer to the diagrams below to learn how to play these chords!

While playing these chords, it's important to keep your fingers up tall and pressed down hard enough to hear each note clearly. If you're having trouble hearing every note sound out clearly, try picking out each individual string to see which one is causing the issue. Rearrange your finger placement and try adjusting the amount of pressure you're applying. Once you've got each chord down, try switching between them while playing. With just these five chords, there is already a great number of songs you can play!
